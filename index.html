I. Classical Cryptography.

I.I Caesar Cipher

One of the earliest and most famous forms of cryptography was developed by Emperor Julius Caesar. During the period of battle, he needed a way to send messages secretly to his general. He found a solution, it was simple but indeed effective. It was to shift each letter in the alphabet by three positions. For example, the letter A became D, letter B became E, and so on. This technique was known as Caesar Cipher.

Emperor Julius Caesar made his messages secret by shifting each letter three positions forward in the alphabets. THe algorithm to encrypt a message works like this:

1.Assign numbers to each other
A->00, B->01, C->02 ,D->03, E->04, F->05, G->06, H->07, I->08, J->09,
K->10, L->11, M->12, N->13, O->14, P->15, Q->16, R->17, S->18, T->19,
U->20, V->21, W->22, X->23, Y->24, Z->25

2.Convert the plaintext into numbers:
For example; if the plaintext is
FIRE , we convert each other to its corresponding number:
05 08 17 04

3.Encrypt the numbers by adding 3 to each: 05 08 17 04 -> (05+3) (08+3) (17+3) (04+03) = 08 11 20 07

4.Take the result modulo 26(to stay within the alphabet range):
08 11 20 07 -> 08(mod26) 11(mod26) 20(mod26) 07(mod26)
-> 08 11 20 07

5.Convert the numbers back to the letters:
08 11 20 07 -> ILUH
Ek=3 (FIRE) = ILUH
However, To decrypt the message, we simply reverse the process by subtracting 3 from each numbers and taking modulo 26 again.

"The Affine Cipher is a more complex encryption method that combines both a shift and a multiplication operation. It uses two keys, one for the shift and another for the multipication. The formular for the encryption is E(x) = (ax+b) mod 26, where'a' and 'b' are the keys while 'x' is a numerical representation of the letters."

I.IV. Transportation

In the fascinating world of cryptography, transposition ciphers stand out as one of the oldest and simplest forms of encryption. Rather than changing the actual letters or symbols (as substitution ciphers do), transposition ciphers rearrange the positions of the characters according to a specific system, creating a ciphertext that hides the original message.
